Prototyping and Validation of Biochar-SODIS Water Purification System

An integrated, low-cost solution combining biochar filtration with solar disinfection for comprehensive water purification

Integrated System Overview

This all-in-one system combines biochar filtration with enhanced solar disinfection (SODIS) to purify water from diverse sources. The integrated approach addresses physical, chemical, and biological contaminants through a multi-stage process using locally available materials and solar energy.

Key Features

  • Integrated Design: Combines multiple purification methods in a single system
  • Biochar Filtration: Uses activated charcoal from local biomass for chemical removal
  • Enhanced SODIS: UV disinfection combined with thermal treatment for pathogen kill
  • Adaptable: Modular design allows customization for different water sources
  • Sustainable: Uses renewable energy and locally sourced materials

Research Hypothesis

The combination of biochar filtration with solar disinfection will:

  1. Remove organic contaminants that shield pathogens from UV radiation
  2. Provide additional adsorption capacity for chemicals that heat/UV cannot break down
  3. Create a synergistic effect where the combined system outperforms either method alone
  4. Reduce reliance on long SODIS exposure times through pre-filtration

Chemical Filtration

Scientific Basis

Zeolite's porous structure has negative charges that attract positive ions. Eggshell calcium carbonate reacts with metals to form insoluble compounds.

Solar Disinfection (SODIS)

  • Transparent container allows sunlight penetration
  • UV-A radiation disrupts pathogen DNA
  • Black-painted base enhances heating (>50°C)
  • Lowers pH <4.5 to improve UV kill rate
  • Requires 6-24 hours of sun exposure

Scientific Basis

UV-A (320-400nm) causes thymine dimer formation in DNA. Heat denatures proteins. Combined effect achieves 4-6 log reduction of pathogens.

Why This Combination Works

  1. Synergistic Effect: Each step targets different contaminants - physical, chemical, and biological
  2. Multi-Barrier Protection: Redundancy ensures safety if one step is less effective
  3. Adaptability: Can be modified for different water quality challenges
  4. Energy Efficiency: Uses gravity flow and solar energy with no electricity

Optimization & Future Improvements

Biochar Enhancement

Test different feedstocks (coconut, bamboo, rice husk) and pyrolysis temperatures to optimize adsorption capacity.

SODIS Boosters

Investigate natural pH reducers (lemon, hibiscus) and reflective surfaces to increase UV exposure.

Modular Design

Develop interchangeable filter cartridges for specific contaminants (fluoride, arsenic, etc.).
